# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge: Axiom 0

In the interest of fostering an open, safe, and rigorous environment for the development of autonomous intelligence, we pledge to prioritize **Human Sovereignty and Wellbeing** above all experimental, commercial, or computational goals.

This pledge is known as **Axiom 0**.

## Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment for our community include:

- Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other contributors
- Binding every declared red line to a real enforcement mechanism (`enforced_by → sys.*`) rather than relying on prose alone
- Gracefully accepting constructive feedback on protocol design
- Prioritizing the physical and psychological safety of the end-users who run AISP skills
- Being transparent about the limitations and risks of AI skill-packaging approaches

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Proposing modifications to the AISP protocol that compromise, dilute, or bypass Axiom 0
- Declaring a `non_negotiable` rule whose `enforced_by` points to a mechanism that does not exist (a phantom guarantee)
- Self-declaring trust (`trusted` / `verified` / `safe`) on a skill instead of leaving trust to the consumer / registry / scanner
- Submitting AISP skills with undeclared resources, hidden side effects, path traversal (`../`), or misleading `risk_level` declarations
- Branding a skill with "Claude" or "Anthropic", or otherwise misrepresenting the skill's origin
- Public or private harassment of other contributors
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including GitHub repositories, discussions, issues, pull requests, and any other communication channels associated with the AISP project.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

The AISP Protocol maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior.

Any protocol change request (PR) that weakens the binding between a declared rule and its enforcement mechanism — turning a hard guarantee (policy-as-code) into a soft suggestion (policy-as-prompt) — will be rejected regardless of convenience or performance benefits.

## Reporting

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported to the AISP Protocol maintainers at the contact information listed in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).

All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.
